\section{SPI Slave Description}
% =============================================================================

\subsection{Overview}
The \texttt{spi\_slave} is a small, synchronous SPI slave IP block designed for use in the IHP SG13G2 process. It exposes eight 8-bit configuration and status registers to an SPI master and provides a secondary parallel read port for on-chip register readback independent of the SPI bus.

\subsection{SPI Protocol}
The core operates in \textbf{SPI Mode 0} (CPOL\,=\,0, CPHA\,=\,0): the master drives data on the falling edge of SCK and the slave samples on the rising edge.

Every transaction consists of two bytes:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Command byte} — the MSB (bit\,7) carries the R/\=W flag: 1\,=\,Read, 0\,=\,Write; bits\,[6:0] carry the 7-bit register address.
  \item \textbf{Data byte} — for a write this is the value to store; for a read the slave shifts out the register value on MISO while MOSI is ignored.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Register Map}
Eight 8-bit registers are addressable at addresses \texttt{0x00}--\texttt{0x07}. A read at any address outside this range returns \texttt{0xFF}. Reset (power-on) values are listed below.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ccc|ccc}
\toprule
Address & Register & Reset value & Address & Register & Reset value \\
\midrule
\texttt{0x00} & \texttt{Register0} & \texttt{0x0A} & \texttt{0x04} & \texttt{Register4} & \texttt{0x0E} \\
\texttt{0x01} & \texttt{Register1} & \texttt{0x0B} & \texttt{0x05} & \texttt{Register5} & \texttt{0x10} \\
\texttt{0x02} & \texttt{Register2} & \texttt{0x0C} & \texttt{0x06} & \texttt{Register6} & \texttt{0x20} \\
\texttt{0x03} & \texttt{Register3} & \texttt{0x0D} & \texttt{0x07} & \texttt{Register7} & \texttt{0x30} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Port Interface}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llp{8cm}}
\toprule
Port & Direction & Description \\
\midrule
\texttt{Clk}       & in  & System clock; all internal logic is synchronous to this domain \\
\texttt{iRST\_N}   & in  & Active-low asynchronous reset \\
\texttt{SCK}       & in  & SPI clock from the master \\
\texttt{MOSI}      & in  & Master-out slave-in data \\
\texttt{SSEL}      & in  & SPI slave select, active low \\
\texttt{MISO}      & out & Master-in slave-out data \\
\texttt{Add2\_in}  & in  & Parallel read port: 8-bit register address \\
\texttt{Data2\_out}& out & Parallel read port: register data (updated every \texttt{Clk} cycle) \\
\texttt{debug}     & out & Sticky OR of state-machine states visited since last reset \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Internal State Machine}
The SPI interface logic is implemented as a Moore state machine with five states clocked by \texttt{Clk}. Asynchronous SPI inputs (SCK, MOSI, SSEL) are first captured through a two-flip-flop metastability synchroniser before entering the state machine.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{clc}
\toprule
State & Description & \texttt{debug} bit \\
\midrule
\texttt{IDLE}    & Waits for SSEL to go low & 0 \\
\texttt{COMMAND} & Shifts in 8-bit command/address byte on SCK rising edges & 1 \\
\texttt{WRITE}   & Shifts in 8-bit data byte; writes register on completion & 2 \\
\texttt{READ}    & Shifts out register value on MISO on SCK falling edges & 3 \\
\texttt{END}     & Waits for SSEL to return high, then returns to IDLE & 4 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{RTL2GDS Flow}
% =============================================================================

The repository includes a \textbf{LibreLane}-based RTL2GDS flow configured for the IHP SG13G2 PDK.

\subsection{Key Flow Files}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{flow/Makefile} -- entry point for LibreLane and OpenROAD commands.
  \item \texttt{flow/config.yaml} -- LibreLane configuration file.
  \item \texttt{flow/run\_flow.sh} -- environment setup and flow wrapper.
  \item \texttt{flow/ihp\_pdk.env.example} -- example PDK environment template.
  \item \texttt{flow/constraint.sdc} -- timing constraint for the \texttt{spi\_slave} clock.
  \item \texttt{flow/place\_route.tcl} -- fallback OpenROAD placement/routing script.
  \item \texttt{flow/spi\_slave\_synth.v} -- Yosys-generated structural Verilog netlist (used in B2B verification).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Running the Flow}
\begin{lstlisting}
cd spi_slave_github_repo/flow
cp ihp_pdk.env.example ihp_pdk.env
# Edit ihp_pdk.env to point to the local IHP PDK installation
./run_flow.sh
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Flow Execution Results}
The RTL2GDS flow was executed from \texttt{flow/} and produced a completed LibreLane/OpenROAD run under \texttt{runs/RUN\_2026-06-07\_13-47-22}. Key outputs include:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{runs/RUN\_2026-06-07\_13-47-22/final/gds/spi\_slave.gds} (803\,KiB, includes seal ring)
  \item \texttt{runs/RUN\_2026-06-07\_13-47-22/final/def/spi\_slave.def} (624\,KiB)
\end{itemize}

The flow ran all 69 steps and completed with warnings only (no errors). A patch was applied to the installed \texttt{librelane} seal-ring script to fix a KLayout API incompatibility with the PDK's Python-wrapper PCells: \texttt{layout.create\_cell} was replaced by \texttt{add\_pcell\_variant} and the die dimensions were corrected from database units (nm) to the \(\mu\)m values expected by the seal-ring PCell.

\section{Back-to-Back Verification}
% =============================================================================

\subsection{Methodology}
Back-to-back (B2B) verification confirms functional equivalence between the RTL source (\texttt{spi\_slave.v}) and the Yosys-synthesised netlist (\texttt{spi\_slave\_synth.v}, in \texttt{flow/}). Both receive identical stimulus and their outputs are compared.

The testbench \texttt{tb\_spi\_slave.v} drives two SPI transactions:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{READ} from Register\,1 (address byte \texttt{0x81}: R/\=W\,=\,1, addr\,=\,\texttt{0x01}). The default reset value \texttt{0x0B} is expected on MISO.
  \item \textbf{WRITE} \texttt{0xFF} to Register\,3 (address byte \texttt{0x03}: R/\=W\,=\,0, addr\,=\,\texttt{0x03}).
\end{enumerate}

The same testbench is compiled twice: once against the RTL (\texttt{spi\_slave.v}) and once with the synthesised netlist substituted in place of the \texttt{`include}.

\subsection{Simulation Commands}
\begin{lstlisting}
# RTL simulation
iverilog -g2012 -o tb_rtl.out tb_spi_slave.v
vvp tb_rtl.out          # produces tb_spi_slave.vcd

# Synthesised netlist simulation (replace include at compile time)
sed 's|`include "spi_slave.v"|`include "flow/spi_slave_synth.v"|g' \
    tb_spi_slave.v \
  | sed 's|tb_spi_slave.vcd|tb_spi_slave_synth.vcd|g' \
  > tb_spi_slave_synth_run.v
iverilog -g2012 -o tb_synth.out tb_spi_slave_synth_run.v
vvp tb_synth.out  # produces tb_spi_slave_synth.vcd
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Results}

All observed signal values at the end of simulation (t\,=\,750\,ns) were identical between RTL and synthesised netlist:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
Signal & RTL & Synth & Status \\
\midrule
\texttt{rst\_n}  & 1    & 1    & MATCH \\
\texttt{ssel}    & 0    & 0    & MATCH \\
\texttt{sck}     & 0    & 0    & MATCH \\
\texttt{mosi}    & 1    & 1    & MATCH \\
\texttt{miso}    & 1    & 1    & MATCH \\
\texttt{data}    & \texttt{0xFF} & \texttt{0xFF} & MATCH \\
\texttt{debug}   & \texttt{0x1F} & \texttt{0x1F} & MATCH \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The \texttt{debug} value \texttt{0x1F} (bits\,0--4 all set) confirms that all five state-machine states (IDLE, COMMAND, WRITE, READ, END) were visited during the simulation. The \texttt{data} output \texttt{0xFF} confirms the WRITE transaction successfully stored \texttt{0xFF} in Register\,3, which is then reflected on the parallel read port (\texttt{Add2\_in}\,=\,3). The \texttt{miso} value 1 corresponds to the LSB of Register\,1 (\texttt{0x0B}), which was the last bit shifted out during the READ transaction.

\section{Conclusions}
% =============================================================================

The \texttt{spi\_slave} design has been verified at two levels:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Functional correctness}: Both a register read and a register write transaction were exercised in simulation, producing the expected MISO output and register update.
  \item \textbf{Synthesis equivalence}: All observable output signals are bit-for-bit identical between the RTL model and the Yosys-generated structural netlist, confirming that synthesis introduced no functional changes.
\end{itemize}

The LibreLane/OpenROAD RTL2GDS flow has been executed successfully, yielding a final GDS for the IHP SG13G2 target process.
